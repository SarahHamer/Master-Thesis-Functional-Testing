NOTE:
The 'Type' field indicates whether the EDIH has received a 'Seal of Excellence', is an EDIH, or a DIH.
- 'EDIH' means the hub is part of the funded European Digital Innovation Hub network. It is co-funded by the European Commission and Member States/Associated Countries.
- 'Seal of Excellence' means the hub was evaluated positively in a European competitive call but is funded exclusively by national or regional resources.
- 'DIH' means digital innovation hubs with similar activities to EDIHs but not connected to the network.

EDIH Name: Malta-EDIH
Title: The creation of the Malta-EDIH as part of the network of European Digital Innovation Hubs
Country: Malta
Description: The project proposed, led by the Malta Digital Innovation Authority, foresees the setting up of a single EDIH in Malta titled: DiHubMT. In line with the call for proposals for the EDIHs, the DiHubMT aims at providing the complete set of services of an EDIH on an open, transparent and non-discriminatory basis and targeted towards SMEs, small midcaps and public sector organisations. The following are the main services that will be offered at the DiHubMT:
- Digital Entrepreneurship Support Services to facilitate digital and green transformation with a focus on existing local competencies in the area, namely, Artificial Intelligence (AI), Cybersecurity and High-Performance Computing (HPC). This also foresees the setting up of a Pilot Entrepreneurship Programme (incubator, pre-accelerator and accelerator) and the provision of specialised services in relation to access to finance.
- Technical ‘Test before Invest’ Support Services via the setting up of a High-Tech Workspace which will feature high-end technologies, namely, AI, Cybersecurity / Information security, HPC, Augmented Reality, Virtual Reality, 3D Printing, Drones and Robotics.
- Digital Skills and Training Development to drive cross-sector upskilling and reskilling digital skills educational initiatives with a focus on advanced digital skills linked to short-term training courses, an apprenticeship programme and a train-the-trainer programme.
-I nnovation Ecosystems and Networking to promote the concept of Innovation Communities and facilitate networking and collaboration with the Digital Transformation Accelerator, the European network of EDIHs, and other relevant stakeholders and local actors, to enable the sharing of best practices and specialist knowledge.
- Digital Innovation Policy and Strategy to support the implementation of policies and bridge the gap between the private sector and the European Commission and Government with the aim of developing evidence-based policy making.

Description: The Mittelstand-Digital Zentrum Franken supports companies in northern Bavaria in designing and implementing digital strategies, tools and workflows for digitalisation and sustainability. The programme covers the use of artificial intelligence in a wide range of business domains as well as the topics of energy and resource efficiency and sustainability management. The centre's showrooms in Ansbach and Nuremberg offer workshops, training courses, consultation hours and other formats to encourage exchange. The centre trains and supports the use of digital tools and workflows for the following applications: - Identifying the potential of artificial intelligence in a wide range of business domains and company sizes - Technical applications of AI, e.g. machine learning, computer vision, quality control and process optimisation - Use of digital assistance systems for knowledge assurance, customer communication and other business applications - Sustainability-oriented risk analyses, sustainability management and reporting as well as supply chain management - Energy balancing and energy management - Improved material efficiency, circular thinking and end-of-life planning Cooperation with third parties is an important factor for the centre. We provide you with vendor-neutral support - at no cost to you. The aim of the centre is to improve the holistic understanding of digital transformation in SMEs and to support the implementation of future-proof digital applications. SMEs are supported in their digital transformation through concrete digital applications, demonstration and testing opportunities, experience spaces, customisable offers for companies and a large number of practical examples. Employees, social partners and managers are actively involved at an early stage in order to introduce new technologies and business systems in an employee-friendly way. One focus of our work is our AI trainer programme, which supports you in the introduction of artificial intelligence (AI) in your company. Specific use cases are used to demonstrate the benefits of AI in the areas of data analysis, assistance systems and intelligent production. In our workshops, we train your employees - also in company-specific formats. Various demonstrators are made available in our roadshow and in our stationary model factories in Cottbus and Wildau. Companies can also train their own AI algorithms in the Open AI Lab. We also address questions about the influence of AI on entrepreneurs and works councils and provide advice on how to organise its introduction.
