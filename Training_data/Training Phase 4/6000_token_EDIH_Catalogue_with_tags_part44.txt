NOTE:
The 'Type' field indicates whether the EDIH has received a 'Seal of Excellence', is an EDIH, or a DIH.
- 'EDIH' means the hub is part of the funded European Digital Innovation Hub network. It is co-funded by the European Commission and Member States/Associated Countries.
- 'Seal of Excellence' means the hub was evaluated positively in a European competitive call but is funded exclusively by national or regional resources.
- 'DIH' means digital innovation hubs with similar activities to EDIHs but not connected to the network.

EDIH Name: MDZLR
Title: Mittelstand-Digital Zentrum Ländliche Regionen
Country: Germany
Description: Als Mittelstand-Digital Zentrum richten wir unseren Fokus gezielt auf kleine und mittlere Unternehmen in ländlichen Regionen und unterstützen sie bei der digitalen Transformation. Mit unserem breitgefächerten Angebot adressieren wir dabei gezielt die Bedürfnisse und Herausforderungen von KMU und unterstützen sie dabei, sich zukunftsfähig und resilient aufzustellen. Wir verfolgen einen menschzentrierten, sozialpartnerschaftlichen und nachhaltigen Ansatz und sind überzeugt, dass Digitalisierung nur gemeinsam gelingen kann. Aus diesem Grund steht bei uns nicht die Technik, sondern der Mensch, der sie nutzt, im Mittelpunkt. Um die anstehenden Transformationsherausforderungen erfolgreich zu meistern, müssen Digitalisierung und Nachhaltigkeit immer zusammengedacht werden, sowohl auf ökologischer, ökonomischer als auch sozialer Ebene. Mit unseren Angeboten wollen wir KMU die Chancen und Möglichkeiten, aber auch konkrete Anwendungsbereiche von Digitalisierung näherbringen. Mit Informations- und Vernetzungsveranstaltungen wollen wir aktuelles Digitalisierungswissen aufbauen und Unternehmen die Möglichkeit geben, sich zu Digitalisierungsthemen auszutauschen und voneinander zu lernen. Unsere Qualifizierungs- und Workshopformate richten sich an alle Mitarbeiter:innen in Unternehmen und vermitteln Know-how und Methoden, um die Digitalisierung im eigenen Unternehmen umzusetzen. Im Rahmen von Projekten unterstützen wir Unternehmen bei der gezielten Umsetzung von Digitalisierungsvorhaben. Unsere Demonstratoren machen digitale Innovationen erlebbar und zeigen die Machbarkeit von Digitalisierungslösungen. Unser Angebot ist für Unternehmen kostenfrei, anbieterneutral und praxisnah. Das Mittelstand-Digital Zentrum Ländliche Regionen gehört zu Mittelstand-Digital. Mit dem Mittelstand-Digital Netzwerk unterstützt das Bundesministerium für Wirtschaft und Klimaschutz die Digitalisierung in kleinen und mittleren Unternehmen und dem Handwerk. Das Mittelstand-Digital Netzwerk bietet mit den Mittelstand-Digital Zentren, der Initiative IT-Sicherheit in der Wirtschaft und Digital Jetzt umfassende Unterstützung bei der Digitalisierung. Kleine und mittlere Unternehmen profitieren von konkreten Praxisbeispielen und passgenauen, anbieterneutralen Angeboten zur Qualifikation und IT-Sicherheit. Das Bundesministerium für Wirtschaft und Klimaschutz ermöglicht die kostenfreie Nutzung und stellt finanzielle Zuschüsse bereit.

Description: The project’s main objective is to establish and develop Digital Innovation Hub Mechanika in town of Peshtera, South Central Region of Bulgaria, which supports SMEs, small-midcaps and public entities in digital transformation through providing services as testing before investing and trainings and consultancy, as well as through providing support to find investment and to find effective collaborations in regional innovation ecosystem. The effect of the project implementation is the increased capacity for digital transformation and innovation activities, including increased innovation potential in the South Central planning region in the field of automation and digitalisation of the footwear and garment industry.

EDIH Name: MicroCyber
Title: MicroCyber
Country: Italy
Description: The MicroCyber EDIH aims at establishing a set of services and infrastructures focusing on cybersecurity, to be offered to Micro and Small, and enterprises (MSE) in the South of Italy while also supporting public entities – both local and central – in understanding their cybersecurity needs. 
MSEs in the South of Italy need to improve their cybersecurity awareness and preparedness, as a key enabler for operating and thriving in the digital economy. Still, these MSEs are often unable to face the costs for accessing resources needed to improve their cyber-preparedness and are not able to find training and capacity building programs tailored to their needs and resources availability.
To respond to these needs, MicroCyber offers a diversified set of services devoted to its target groups, including assessment of digital and cyber maturity and relevant needs, cyber-range activities to explore and test cybersecurity scenarios and solutions, personalised skills and training activities, networking, also via the involvement in ecosystems and events, as well as support services to access microfinance tools and other financial resources, which the MicroCyber EDIH is perfectly positioned to deliver leveraging the unique expertise of its Coordinator, Ente Nazionale per il Microcredito (ENM).
At the same time, the MicroCyber can build on the unique combination of expertise available within its consortium, with public entities, universities, large SMEs associations, research consortia, private consultancy companies, all equally committed to deliver the highest quality in services, while offering networking and collaboration with the best digital expertises in Europe, via other EDIHs, the Digital Transformation Accelerator, and a variety of other networks in the digital domain available at the local and national level.

EDIH Name: MIDAS
Title: Manufacturing Innovation Digital Advancement Services Hub
Country: Türkiye
Description: MIDAS is dedicated to transforming the manufacturing sector through comprehensive digital and green innovation initiatives, with a primary focus on supporting Small and Medium-sized Enterprises (SMEs) within Türkiye and the broader European Union framework. By establishing a European Digital Innovation Hub (EDIH) in Türkiye, MIDAS bridges the gap between cutting-edge digital technologies and the evolving needs of the manufacturing industry. The hub offers a wide array of services designed to enhance digital capabilities, foster innovation, and drive sustainable growth among SMEs.
Key Objectives and Services
1. Supporting Digital and Green Transformation

Digital Maturity Assessments: Evaluate the digital capabilities of 100 SMEs using advanced tools like DMA and SIRI to create tailored transformation strategies.
Training Programs: Enhance digital literacy and skills in AI, Big Data, Machine Learning, and Web 3.0 through targeted training initiatives.
Consultancy and Mentorship: Provide personalized consultancy to develop Digital Maturity Plans, access funding opportunities, and integrate innovative solutions.
Pre-Investment Testing: Offer a dedicated demo hub with the latest digital infrastructure, enabling SMEs to test and adopt suitable technologies before significant investments.

2. Increasing Organisational and Financial Capacities


Comprehensive Support Package: Provide access to digital tools that improve organizational efficiency, enhance marketing strategies, drive R&D activities, and develop high-quality products while reducing costs.


Funding Access: Assist SMEs in navigating financial barriers by connecting them with funding sources such as SME loans, venture capital, angel investors, and government incentives through partnerships with ISTKA and ARI.


Workshops and Webinars: Conduct interactive workshops and webinars to share knowledge, best practices, and build a robust innovation ecosystem, engaging over 600 participants and providing hands-on training for 64 SMEs.


3. Collaborating with the European Digital Innovation Hubs Network

Networking and Collaboration: Establish strong connections with existing EDIHs across Europe, such as ITBaltic, to foster long-term partnerships and collaborative projects, enabling resource sharing and joint innovation initiatives.
Stakeholder Engagement: Unite key industries, organizations, regulatory bodies, startups, and SMEs to create a vibrant collaborative environment and produce policy recommendation papers based on empirical evidence.
International Research and Innovation Calls: Facilitate participation in prestigious programs like Horizon Europe, Eurostars, Eureka, Eranet, Digital Europe Program, TÜBİTAK, and KOSGEB, thereby increasing the visibility and competitiveness of Turkish SMEs on a global scale.

EDIH Name: MIGHTY EDIH
Title: Mid Sweden Industry and GovTech EDIH
Country: Sweden
Description: MIGHTY EDIH (MIGHTY) will contribute to the European digital and green transition of SMEs, small-midcaps and public entities (Target groups), with special focus on Manufacturing industry and Govtech all over Europe. Building on a vast experience of bringing these sectors together in already ongoing cross-border collaborations, this combination will be a strong driver for innovative and silo-breaking digital and green transition of local companies and public actors. MIGHTY is based in two cohesive NUTS-2 regions (North Middle Sweden and Middle Norrland) covering 30 % of Sweden's area. Twinning together the regional S3-strategies for smart specialization with the European strategic goals for digital and green transition, MIGHTY will contribute from both a bottom-up and top-down perspective to make Europe climate neutral by 2050 in accordance with the Paris Agreement by strengthen the resilience and competitiveness of European industry and speed up the uptake and digitalisation within the public sector. Project partners will function as One-Stop-Shop, ensuring wide-spread local presence offering all EDIH-services foreseen in all regions in the spirit that all SMEs should have access to an EDIH within working distance. Cooperation with regional Industrial Networks is key in reach out to manufacturing SME in all regions. MIGHTY is built on long time established and well recognized non-profit-organizations already functioning as regional digital innovation hubs and science parks. This consortium is coordinated by RISE Research Institutes of Sweden and has a strong backing from the regions as well as from the four regional universities. Joining forces in MIGHTY means the consortium will evolve further and faster to better support target groups by leveraging and exchanging services, experience, and know-how of each other and of EDIHs across Europe, supporting the goals in DIGITAL and European Green Deal.

EDIH Name: Minalogic
Title: Minalogic
Country: France
Description: Minalogic is a global innovation cluster for digital technologies serving France’s Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes region.
The cluster supports the region’s leading innovators by facilitating networking, fostering collaborative R&D, and providing companies with personalized assistance throughout all phases of business growth. The products and services developed by our members address all industries, from ICT and healthcare to energy, advanced manufacturing and mobility. Minalogic’s roadmap concentrates on 11 markets considered as strategic for our members: ICT, Healthcare, Energy & Environment, Advanced manufacturing, Agrifood & Foodtech, Aerospace & Defense, Mobility & Transportation; Finance & Insurance; Smart Cities & Buildings, Trade & distribution.
Minalogic was founded in 2005 and today boasts approximately 400 members, including 350 companies. Our companies are developing components or integrating solutions/systems related to sensors, cameras, communication, IoT and cyber-physical systems, data analytics and artificial intelligence.
The cluster has certified more than 586 projects that have secured total government funding of €881 million of the more than €2,2 billion in total R&D spending these projects represent. The 72 projects completed to date have resulted in 86 products (either on the market or in the process of being prepared for market release) and generated €3,6 billion in revenue.
Minalogic was awarded with Gold Label for Cluster Excellence for 2nd time in 2016 assessing of the high quality of its management and the impact of its activities, thanks to the cluster’s highly motivated, competent and committed team of 19 people.
Minalogic is founding member of the Silicon Europe Alliance. In the Silicon Europe Alliance 12 leading European clusters for innovative electronics & software technologies bring together the technological expertise and resources of Europe’s leading players in micro- and nanoelectronics, photonics and artificial intelligence three of the EC recognised “Key Enabling Technologies”. This “cluster of clusters” represents over 2 000 members (over 75% SMEs) and more than 250,000 jobs. This makes the Silicon Europe Alliance one of the biggest technology clusters in the world.
